02 Our Thesis

We are not automating the old shop.
We are building its replacement.

The capacity we are losing to closing businesses can't come back by simply training machinists faster or by moving quoting online. The production itself needs to be rebuilt.

A shop built around human judgment at every step cannot be automated into one that runs without it, not economically, not practically, not quickly. The entire operation has to be designed for autonomy from the first cut. That means building new from the ground up, not incrementally retrofitting the old.

The hard part was never the machines. It is knowing how to make each part. The feeds, the speeds, how a tool wears, what to do when a cut goes wrong, the setups, the fixtures, the finishing. Every part we make adds to that knowledge, and the knowledge compounds.

NEXT is not just a job shop that runs faster. It is the production capacity that absorbs work from shops that are closing and overflow from shops that are not, and it gets better with every part it makes.

03 The Window

Five forces are converging right now.

There is a narrow window to rebuild America's production base before we lose the knowledge and capacity to do so.

1

Reshoring mandate

National policy and market demand are pushing to rebuild domestic production capacity. The political will exists across both parties.

2

Structural labor shortage

Skilled machinists are retiring faster than replacements emerge. There is no near-term workforce solution.

3

Automation economics crossed the threshold

Hardware costs and integration maturity now make commercially viable autonomous production achievable for the first time.

4

AI unlocks the orchestration layer

AI capabilities are now advanced enough to coordinate machines, software, and workflows as one cohesive system.

5

Unprecedented market consolidation

A wave of retiring shop owners is forcing billions in orphaned production to consolidate. The question isn't whether it consolidates. It's whether it consolidates into modern domestic facilities or disappears overseas.
