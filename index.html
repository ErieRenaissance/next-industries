02 Why Now

Three things are true at the same time.

The conditions to rebuild domestic production are here right now. They will not stay this aligned for long.

1

Reshoring is funded, not just discussed

Policy and customer demand are both pulling production back onshore, and the support holds across both parties.

2

The labor gap has no fix coming

The machinists retiring took decades to train. Nothing is replacing them at the rate they are leaving.

3

Retiring owners are forcing a sell-off

Thousands of shop owners are aging out with no successor. Billions in production is about to change hands. It either consolidates into modern domestic capacity, or it disappears.

04 What We're Building

This is not a concept.
We're building it in Erie.

Our first production cell runs on a single orchestration layer that coordinates the machine, the robotics, and the inspection as one system. The launch scope is deliberately narrow, so the system proves out before it scales.

1

A first cell, live in Erie

A 5-axis CNC at the center, with robotic loading and material movement and automated inspection built around it.

Built to scale cell by cell
